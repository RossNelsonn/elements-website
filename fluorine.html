FLUORINE

It is a fairly rare element in the universe. Fluorine is a pale yellow, highly corrosive, flammable gas, with a pungent smell.

How is Fluorine used today?

Compounds, such as sodium fluoride, are used in toothpaste and drinking water to keep teeth healthy. Hydrofluoric acid is used to etch the glass in light bulbs. Fluorine is used in rocket fuels and in processing nuclear fuel.
