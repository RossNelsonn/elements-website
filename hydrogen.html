HYDROGEN

Hydrogen is the first element in the periodic table and it is a colourless, odorless, and tasteless gas.

Symbol Atomic Number Atomic Weight State Discovered By
H 1 1.01 Gas Henry Cavendish (1766)

Where is Hydrogen found?

The most common place to find hydrogen on earth is in water. The Sun is made up of mostly hydrogen.

How is hydrogen used today?

It is used to make ammonia for fertilizers and refining metals. Hydrogen is also used as a rocket fuel where liquid hydrogen is combined with liquid oxygen to produce a powerful explosion.

Interesting Facts about Hydrogen

Hydrogen makes up over 90 percent of all the atoms in the universe. It becomes a liquid at very low temperature and high pressure. Under extremely high pressure, it can become a liquid metal.
