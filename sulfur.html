SULPHUR

Sulphur is classified as a nonmetal. Under normal conditions, it is a pale yellow solid, which is soft and odourless. The gas, hydrogen sulphide, is famous for having the strong smell of rotten eggs.

Where is Sulphur found?

Sulphur can be found in a number of areas on Earth including volcanic emissions and hot springs.

How is Sulphur used today?

The majority of sulfur is used to make the chemical sulfuric acid. It is also used to make car batteries, fertilizer, gun powder, matches, refine oil, process water, and to extract minerals.

Interesting Facts about Sulphur

Sulphur burns with a blue flame, but melts into a blood-red liquid.
